About AFANA

Why we exist and what we're building.

AFANA Animal Futures was founded on a simple belief: that animals raised for food deserve better, and that improving their welfare makes everything around them better too.

Our Story

Born out of a gap nobody was filling.

Nigeria has one of the largest populations of farmed animals in Africa. Millions of people depend on these animals for food, income, and livelihoods. Yet animal welfare, the basic question of how these animals are living, was rarely part of the conversation.

Farhan and Kaosarah founded AFANA Animal Futures in Nigeria to begin filling that gap. Not with assumptions, but with research. Not from the outside, but working directly alongside the farming communities at the centre of the issue.

AFANA is rooted in two ideas: trust and Africa. Inspired by the word "Amana," meaning trust, our name reflects the values that guide our work through partnership, evidence, and a commitment to building a more humane and sustainable future.

We are a young organisation. We are still building. But we are building on something solid: a commitment to evidence, to community, and to the animals at the heart of our work.

Why It Matters

Poor welfare costs everyone, not just the animals.

Nigeria is home to over 180 million farmed animals. Poultry and livestock farming employs millions of Nigerians and is central to national food security, yet welfare is rarely factored into how farms are run, financed, or supported.1

The consequences are real. Poor welfare conditions are directly linked to higher disease burden, lower farm productivity, greater food safety risks, and weaker farmer incomes. Improving welfare isn't a luxury; it's foundational to a more resilient food system.2

That's why our work isn't just about animals. It's about the farmers who depend on them, the communities who eat what they produce, and the environment we all share.

¹ Federal Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development — Nigeria livestock sector overview.
² World Organisation for Animal Health (WOAH) — Animal welfare and food security linkages, 2023.

Our Approach

The One Health framework.

Everything we do is grounded in the understanding that animal health, human health, and environmental health are inseparable. This is what the global health community calls "One Health," and it's the lens through which AFANA sees its work.

🐔

Animals

Every farmed animal has the capacity to suffer and to thrive. Our starting point is always the welfare of the animals themselves, their health, behaviour, and living conditions.

🧑‍🌾

People

The farmers, veterinarians, and communities who work with and around animals every day are essential partners, not just in implementing change, but in designing it.

🌍

Environment

Better animal welfare is also better for the land, the soil, and the ecosystems that sustain farming communities across Nigeria and Africa.

What Drives Us

Our values

Four principles that shape every decision we make.

Evidence-led

We only act on what we can measure. Research comes first and shapes everything that follows.

Community-centred

Solutions are built with the people closest to the problem, not handed down to them.

Practical

We focus on changes that work in the real conditions of Nigerian farming: affordable, scalable, and sustainable.

Transparent

We share what we learn, including both successes and failures, because the whole movement benefits from open knowledge.
