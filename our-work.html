Our Work

Research-driven, community-built programmes.

Our work is organised into three strategic pillars — each reinforcing the others, all aimed at lasting improvements in farmed animal welfare in Nigeria.

Pillar One

Research & Evidence

Before we can improve conditions for farmed animals, we need to understand what those conditions actually are. We conduct rigorous, context-specific research into animal welfare on Nigerian farms — and how welfare connects to human health, farmer livelihoods, and the environment.

We work with universities, veterinary institutions, and international partners to ground our findings in credible evidence, and we share everything we learn openly.

  • → On-farm welfare assessments across Kaduna State
  • → One Health research partnerships with academic institutions
  • → Open publication of findings and methodology
  • → Evidence base to guide future programme design
Pillar Two

Advocacy & Capacity Building

Research alone doesn't change anything. We turn evidence into action — designing interventions that are practical for real farmers, and building the skills and knowledge of the people who care for animals every day.

We work with farmers, veterinarians, and animal health paraprofessionals to implement better practices, and we engage with institutions and policymakers to create the conditions for systemic change.

  • → Training for farmers, vets and paraprofessionals
  • → Practical, low-cost welfare interventions on farms
  • → Policy and institutional engagement at state level
  • → Tools and resources designed for local contexts
Pillar Three

Community & Public Engagement

Long-term change requires public understanding and community ownership. We build partnerships with local groups, community leaders, and civil society organisations — and we help the wider public understand why animal welfare matters, both for animals and for people.

We also share our learning with other animal welfare organisations across Africa, contributing to a growing continental movement.

  • → Partnerships with local groups and institutions
  • → Public education on animal sentience and welfare
  • → Shared learning with peer organisations across Africa
  • → Media and communications to shift public attitudes
